Visitors come from all over the globe to witness the park's 1,200 lilac bushes, which show 500 species of lilacs in a floral masterpiece of the darkest purples and purest whites, each May during the Annual Lilac Festival in Rochester's Highland Park. The Rochester Lilac Celebration, now in its 124th year, is the largest free festival of its type. The Rochester Lilac Festival, which takes place every year in Highland Park, draws almost half a million tourists from all over the world. Every May, the festival kicks up on Mother's Day weekend.

The Rochester Lilac Festival has plenty of live music ranging from national touring performers to area players, in addition to the flowers. There is also an award-winning Art in the Park art show, as well as a Lilac 5K/10K, and a Special Events Tent with a Wine Expo and Craft Beer Expo.

The Rochester International Jazz Festival has swiftly established itself as one of the country's most popular and well-respected music events. Every summer, tourists from all over the United States and the world go to this city-wide music event. Over the course of the nine-day festival, over 1,500 musicians play in over 300 performances on both open-air stages and ticketed interior locations. The venues, all within walking distance of one other in and around Rochester's east end cultural area.

The concerts showcase a diverse range of musical styles and a combination of local, national, and international performers. Food, drinks, and other special events will be available to visitors. Artists and guests are invited to the event at night.
